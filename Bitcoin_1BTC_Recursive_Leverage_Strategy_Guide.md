## SECTION 2: STRATEGY MECHANICS

### How Modified Recursive Leverage Works

**The Core Concept:**
As BTC price appreciates, your collateral value increases. This increased collateral allows you to borrow more. Using these new loans to buy more BTC creates exponential accumulation instead of linear DCA.

**Mathematical Model:**
```
Phase 1 (Month 0, BTC $70K):
  - Borrow: $20,000 USDT
  - Buy: 0.286 BTC (at $70K)
  - Holding: 0.1717 + 0.286 = 0.458 BTC
  - Debt: $20,000
  - LTV: 65%

Phase 2 (Month 6, BTC appreciates to $95K):
  - Collateral value: 0.458 BTC × $95K = $43,510
  - Available borrow (55% LTV): $43,510 × 55% = $23,931
  - Outstanding debt: $20,000
  - New borrow capacity: $3,931 + existing margin
  - Action: Borrow $15,000 (conservative)
  - Buy: 0.158 BTC (at $95K)
  - New Holding: 0.616 BTC
  - New Debt: $35,000
  - New LTV: 50%

Phase 3 (Month 12, BTC appreciates to $120K):
  - Collateral value: 0.616 BTC × $120K = $73,920
  - Available borrow (55% LTV): $73,920 × 55% = $40,656
  - Outstanding debt: $35,000
  - New borrow capacity: $5,656
  - Action: Borrow $10,000 (conservative)
  - Buy: 0.083 BTC (at $120K)
  - New Holding: 0.699 BTC
  - New Debt: $45,000
  - New LTV: 48%

Phase 4 (Month 16-20, BTC $130K+):
  - Final push with DCA or micro-loan
  - Accumulate: 0.301 BTC remaining
  - Final Holding: 1.0 BTC achieved!
```

**Key Safety Rules:**

## SECTION 5: RISK MANAGEMENT & SAFETY PROTOCOLS

### Understanding LTV (Loan-to-Value)

LTV = (Total Debt / Total Collateral Value) × 100

**Safety Levels:**
```
0-40% LTV:    Ultra Safe (you're fine even with 50% crash)
40-50% LTV:   Safe (you're fine with 30% crash)
50-60% LTV:   Acceptable (you're fine with 20% crash) ← TARGET RANGE
60-70% LTV:   Risky (liquidation possible with 15% crash)
70%+ LTV:     Danger Zone (liquidation imminent at any crash)
```

**Your Target: Maintain 55% LTV maximum at all times**

### Liquidation Cascade Risk

**What is it?**
When you have multiple loans and BTC crashes, all loans can be liquidated at the SAME TIME, causing:
1. Forced selling of your collateral at loss
2. Still owing the difference to exchange
3. Underwater position you can't recover from

**Example of Cascade (What to AVOID):**
```
Scenario: You have 3 loans, holding 0.8 BTC
BTC price: $120K → crashes to $75K (37% drop)

Before crash:
- 0.8 BTC × $120K = $96K collateral
- Debt: $45K
- LTV: 47% (safe)

After crash:
- 0.8 BTC × $75K = $60K collateral
- Debt: still $45K
- LTV: 75% (liquidation triggered!)
- Exchange force-liquidates: sells 0.75 BTC at $75K = $56,250
- You now have: 0.05 BTC + owe $11,250 (after liquidation sale)
- RESULT: Rekt. Lost most collateral, still in debt.
```

**How to Prevent:**
1. **Keep LTV ≤55%** (gives 30% crash buffer) ✓
2. **Emergency fund $15K** (to repay Loan #3 at first sign of crash) ✓
3. **Weekly monitoring** (catch problems early) ✓
4. **Exit trigger: BTC <$52K** (repay everything if breached) ✓
5. **Max 3 loans** (fewer loans = fewer cascade points) ✓

### Exit Strategy (If Things Go Wrong)
